\begin{abstract}
We describe a layered safety framework for clinical LLMs that integrates deterministic rule enforcement, retrieval-grounded reasoning, and uncertainty calibration with abstention. This guardrail stack aims to improve factuality, safety, and calibration while preserving latency suitable for real-time clinical decision support. We present ablations across synthetic and public benchmarks demonstrating consistent improvements in reliability and interpretability.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Large-language models offer potential in clinical settings but require rigorous safety mechanisms to ensure reliability. We design a modular guardrail system emphasizing transparency, calibration, and rule compliance.

\section{Related Work}
