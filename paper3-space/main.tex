\begin{abstract}
We adapt a clinical AI copilot architecture for astronaut health monitoring during long-duration space missions. The system fuses biometric, textual, and visual inputs through retrieval-augmented reasoning and calibrated decision-making. Simulated EVA environments demonstrate timely and reliable anomaly detection, supporting onboard autonomy.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Space medicine requires autonomous, multimodal AI systems capable of monitoring health metrics and issuing early alerts. \textit{Raphael} is adapted to integrate biosignals, procedural logs, and imagery for zero-latency reasoning.

\section{Related Work}
Prior biomedical AI frameworks and human-in-the-loop LLM architectures have inspired domain transfer to extraterrestrial settings.
