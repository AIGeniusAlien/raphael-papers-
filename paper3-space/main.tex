\begin{abstract}
We adapt a clinically oriented copilot to space-medicine scenarios by integrating biometric streams, procedure logs, and image inputs with retrieval-grounded reasoning. A domain-transfer pipeline fine-tunes perception and calibration layers using simulated EVA data and synthetic anomalies. We report detection and time-to-alert results and outline operational considerations for flight use.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Long-duration missions require autonomous health support. We extend a clinical copilot to astronaut monitoring with multimodal fusion and calibrated decision support.

\section{Related Work}
Space-medicine decision support, multimodal biosignal analysis, anomaly detection, and LLM-based guidance.

\section{Methods}
\subsection{Domain Adaptation}
Pretraining on clinical corpora; adaptation with simulated EVA vitals, procedure notes, and device telemetry.
\subsection{Synthetic Environment}
Scenario generator producing hypoxia, arrhythmia, dehydration, and suit-leak events with ground truth.
\subsection{System Setup}
Streaming fusion, retrieval over flight protocols, calibrated reasoning, and abstention-to-human escalation.
